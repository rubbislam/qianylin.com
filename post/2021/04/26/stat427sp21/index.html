Course Description

Students will learn useful and interesting ideas from the probability theory and its applications which are closely related to a variety of fields in life, including epidemiology, genetics, economics, finance, business, and engineering. Together with other mathematical tools such as calculus and combinatories, students will develop the skills to apply the probability theory to solve everyday problems.

Course Objectives

Understand the concepts of counting, events, samples, and probability. Distinguish discrete and continuous random variables, familiar with distributions like Binomal, Poisson, Exponential, Normal, Gamma distribution. Develop the skills to compute conditional probability/distribution, joint distribution, expectation, variances. Eventually, students are able to apply probability limit theorems to solve reality problems.
